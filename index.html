The Moroccan babouche is a traditional slipper that has been a staple of Moroccan culture for centuries. Known for its distinctive pointed toe and soft leather construction, the babouche is both comfortable and stylish. These slippers are often handmade by skilled artisans using high-quality leather and are available in a variety of colors and designs. They are not only popular in Morocco but have also gained international recognition for their unique design and craftsmanship. Whether worn casually or as part of a traditional outfit, the Moroccan babouche is a symbol of the country's rich cultural heritage.

Dar Dbagh in Marrakech is a historic tannery where skilled artisans transform raw hides into high-quality leather using traditional methods. Visitors can witness the entire tanning process, from soaking the hides in natural dyes to drying and finishing the leather. This cultural landmark offers a unique glimpse into the age-old craft of leather-making and the vibrant atmosphere of Marrakech's medina. The tannery is an integral part of the city's heritage, reflecting the rich history and craftsmanship that has been passed down through generations. Exploring Dar Dbagh provides a fascinating insight into the traditional techniques and the vibrant community that sustains this ancient trade. The tannery district is a bustling area filled with the sights and sounds of leather production, making it a must-visit for anyone interested in the cultural and historical aspects of Marrakech. The experience is not only educational but also a sensory journey, with the vivid colors of the dyes and the distinctive smells of the tanning process creating a memorable visit.

The process of making a Moroccan babouche is a traditional craft
that requires high skill and precision. It begins with selecting high-quality leather, which is then cut into the required shapes for the upper and lower parts. The leather is dyed in desired colors using natural or synthetic dyes. The upper and lower parts are carefully stitched together to ensure durability and comfort. Finally, the babouche is shaped using special molds, and finishing touches such as polishing and decorations are added. This traditional process ensures that each pair of babouches is unique and of high quality.
